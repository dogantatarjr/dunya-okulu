Harms of Dirty Water

Water scarcity affects more than 40 percent of people, an alarming number that is predicted to rise as temperatures rise. Although 2.1 billion people have improved water sanitation since 1990, dwindling drinking water resources are affecting every continent.
In 2015, 4.5 billion people received safely managed sanitation services (with adequately disposed of or treated faeces) and 2.3 billion people lacked even basic sanitation.

Substances such as arsenic, barium, benzene, cadmium, carbon tetrachloride, chlorine, chlorobenzene, chloroform, chromium, copper, dichlorethylene, ethylenedibromide, fluoride, heptachlor, which are at risk of contamination with water, tumor formation, cramps and spasms, nerve blockage, cancer, leukemia, anemia, It causes many health problems such as stomach, liver and kidney diseases, Wilson's disease. Drinking dirty water poses a great threat to human health, so water must be purified from all these substances before it is consumed.

Harm to Health

According to the research of the World Health Organization, approximately 2 billion people claim that drinking water mixed with sewage water is a direct threat to human health.

Access to Clean Water is Every Child's Right!

Access to clean water is every child's right! When a child has access to safe water at home, they have the chance to live well, learn, play and live their childhood in fairness. Clean water even helps with the education of children: when children are not busy carrying water to their homes, they have time to go to school. If the school has clean water, hygienic toilet and soap for hand washing, children will have a healthy learning environment.

How is Sanitation Done?

Chemical treatment is formed by the addition of various chemicals in the fast and slow mixing units of the waste water and the precipitation of these chemicals by reacting with the pollutants in the waste water.

Biological treatment takes place by biodegradation of domestic or industrial wastewater with the help of oxygenated or oxygenless bacteria.

Physical treatment is the physical removal of oil and coarse wastes in the wastewater by means of mechanical processes, without using any chemicals or bacteria, with screens, oil scraper pallets and similar devices.

Underwater Life

230 thousand creatures live under the sea. Scientists, who counted all living things in the world's seas, announced that the total number of living things in the oceans reached 230 thousand with the discovery of 178 new species last year. But litter dumped in the sea, oil exploration at sea, chemical fertilizers and pesticides, etc. For reasons, many water areas are polluted. And the creatures living under water are the most affected by these events.

What we can do to prevent water pollution;

In order to prevent water pollution, it is necessary to raise awareness of the society about this issue. At the same time, it is necessary to develop systems that prevent large industrial enterprises from dumping their wastes directly to the sea, and to use technologies that separate and purify wastes.
